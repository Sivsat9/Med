Based on your current medical history, it seems that your risk of developing diabetes is relatively low, which is good news. However, it doesn't mean we should be complacent. Lifestyle modifications can play a vital role in maintaining this low risk.

1. Exercise Recommendations: Regular physical activity enhances insulin sensitivity. Types of exercise that may be beneficial include aerobic activities like jogging, biking, swimming, and strength training exercises such as weight lifting. Please consider incorporating both types in your workout routine.

2. Duration and Frequency: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity per week, spread over at least three days. Also, perform strength training exercises at least 2 days a week.

3. Dietary Recommendations: Aim to include plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and foods with a low glycemic index (GI). Low GI foods cause a slower, lower increase in blood sugar levels, and these include whole grain bread, oats, legumes, and non-starchy vegetables.
